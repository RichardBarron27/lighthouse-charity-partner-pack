# Important Safety Information

---

## This extension is NOT a substitute for emergency services.

## If someone is in immediate danger, call 999 or 112 now.

---

## What Lighthouse Does

Lighthouse is a support tool. It watches AI chatbot conversations locally on the device and gently shows helpline numbers when it detects signs of distress. It is designed to point people towards real human support at the moment they may need it most.

## What Lighthouse Does NOT Do

- It does **not** monitor people or report to anyone
- It does **not** alert staff, parents, carers, or any third party
- It does **not** replace professional mental health support
- It does **not** diagnose any condition
- It does **not** intervene in a crisis — it provides information only
- It does **not** contact emergency services on anyone's behalf

## Important Limitations

- Lighthouse uses pattern matching, not artificial intelligence. It looks for specific words and phrases. It may sometimes show helplines when they are not needed, and it may sometimes miss signs of distress that a trained person would notice.
- It only works on the 12 AI chatbot platforms it supports. It does not monitor other websites, messaging apps, or social media.
- It only works when the browser is open and the person is on a supported platform.
- It can be snoozed or dismissed by the person using it. This is by design — it respects the user's choice.

## Emergency Contacts

If someone is in immediate danger of harming themselves or others:

| Service | Number | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Emergency Services** | **999** | Immediate danger to life |
| **Emergency Services (EU)** | **112** | Immediate danger to life |
| **Samaritans** | **116 123** | Emotional distress, any time, 24/7 |
| **Childline** | **0800 1111** | Young people under 19, 24/7 |
| **Crisis Text Line** | **Text HELLO to 85258** | Anyone in crisis, 24/7 |

## For Organisations Deploying Lighthouse

If you are a charity, school, care home, or other organisation distributing Lighthouse:

1. **Lighthouse is one layer of support, not the only layer.** It should sit alongside your existing safeguarding policies, not replace them.
2. **Staff training matters.** People who support vulnerable individuals should be trained to recognise signs of distress and know how to respond. Lighthouse does not remove that need.
3. **Tell people it is installed.** Transparency builds trust. Let people know the extension is there to help, not to spy.
4. **Have a response plan.** If someone tells you they saw the Lighthouse helpline panel, treat that as a conversation starter, not an alarm. Ask how they are. Listen.

## Designed with Care

Lighthouse was built by Red Specter Security Research with input from safeguarding frameworks including CEOP, NSPCC, and IWF guidance. It is designed to be gentle, respectful, and private.

No data is collected. No messages are stored. No one is watched.

It simply says: help is there if you need it.
